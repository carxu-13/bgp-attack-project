Detect suspicious BGP events where a narrowly targeted service prefix is originated by an unexpected ASN in a way that appears intended to block, discard, or misdirect traffic rather than deliver it normally. Build a baseline of expected origins, usual announcement stability, and normal authorization state for the monitored service prefixes. Flag sudden origin changes affecting exact service prefixes or unusually specific targeted announcements that deviate from the baseline, especially when the event is short-lived, externally visible, and inconsistent with expected route authorization data. Increase confidence when the anomaly is highly specific to one service or small set of prefixes rather than part of a broad routing change, suggesting deliberate targeting. Suppress alerts for approved origin migration, temporary maintenance, planned provider changes, and benign measurement artifacts.
