Detect suspicious BGP events consistent with interception rather than simple disruption. Focus on stable prefixes whose routes begin traversing a new unexpected intermediary AS path while still appearing to preserve reachability toward the historical destination. Build a baseline of normal origin ASNs, common AS-path suffixes, and typical upstream relationships for each monitored prefix. Flag cases where a previously stable route suddenly acquires a new intermediary ASN or suspicious path segment that was not present in the baseline, especially when the historical destination ASN or a familiar destination suffix is still visible near the end of the path. Increase confidence when the new route is adopted across multiple peers or collectors within a short time window, indicating that traffic is being successfully attracted rather than merely disrupted. Suppress alerts for approved maintenance, known multihoming behavior, expected provider changes, and isolated collector artifacts.
