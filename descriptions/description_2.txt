Detect suspicious BGP events where a network begins announcing a large set of more-specific prefixes that override legitimate broader routes and attract traffic at scale. Build a baseline of expected origin ASNs, normal prefix granularity, and common announcement patterns for legitimate aggregates. Flag cases where numerous more-specific announcements suddenly appear from an unexpected part of the topology, especially when the announcements are more specific than the legitimate covering routes and therefore likely to win under longest-prefix-match behavior. Increase confidence when the anomalous routes rapidly propagate through a major upstream or become visible across many peers or collectors in a short time window. Suppress alerts for documented deaggregation, legitimate traffic engineering, customer-authorized announcements, and known operational maintenance events.
