Detect suspicious BGP events where a network begins announcing illegitimate more-specific prefixes that are contained within legitimate broader prefixes originated by another network. Build a baseline of expected origin ASNs for legitimate aggregates, along with normal deaggregation patterns and any approved multi-origin exceptions. Flag cases where a newly announced more-specific prefix falls within a stable legitimate covering prefix but is originated by an ASN that does not match the expected origin for that aggregate. Increase confidence when the more-specific route rapidly propagates across multiple peers or collectors and would be preferred over the legitimate aggregate under standard longest-prefix-match behavior, creating the potential for traffic diversion or widespread disruption. Suppress alerts for approved deaggregation, legitimate traffic engineering, expected customer announcements, and other documented routing changes that intentionally produce more-specific routes.
