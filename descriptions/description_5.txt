Detect suspicious BGP events where a network begins announcing illegitimate more-specific prefixes that are contained within a legitimate broader prefix originated by another network. Build a baseline of expected origin ASNs for legitimate aggregates, then flag cases where a newly announced more-specific prefix has an origin ASN that does not match the expected origin for the covering prefix. Increase confidence when the more-specific route rapidly propagates across multiple peers or collectors and would be preferred over the legitimate aggregate under longest-prefix-match behavior. Suppress alerts for approved deaggregation, legitimate traffic engineering, and expected multi-origin routing arrangements.
